What Does it Do?

The All Sky 6 Fireball Camera System has been designed from the ground up with one primary goal — obtain good scientific results from a low cost-effective device and make it easy to setup and operate a fireball camera station.

Included with the device is everything you need to get up and running in less than an hour. This is a turnkey solution. Once the device is powered, connected to your network and placed outside, it will automatically record the skies 24 hours a day. When the software detects motion, it attempts to identify the source. When potential fireballs are recorded, the device automatically calibrates and reduces the videos and extracts precise scientific measurements relating to the event. The video and data is uploaded to the American Meteor Society and the International Meteor Organization fireball database and aggregated with other videos and witness reports for the same event. Of course, you will be credited as the operator of the camera in all the publications (online and paper) and you will have a privilege access to all the data related to the fireball detected by your device.

All the information obtained from our network is used to solve:

  1. The 3D atmospheric trajectory of the fireball
  2. The velocity of the fireball
  3. The magnitude & light curve of the fireball
  4. The pre-atmospheric mass estimate for the meteoroid that caused the fireball
  5. The orbit of the meteoroid prior to hitting Earth

Once determined, these solutions along with the videos are published in near real time on the American Meteor Society and the International Meteor Organization websites. Our fireball database is the most comprehensive catalog of fireball events in the world. Videos collected with these devices will be used to extend the AMS fireball database with hard scientific observations.

Mini Computer

The AllSky6 device contains a Mini Computer Q190S with Celeron j1900 Processor Quad core (2.0 GHz, 8GB RAM 64GB SSD WIFI, Dual NIC Mini PC with Serial Port). The Mini Computer is housed in a custom cut acrylic case that includes a power supply, network switches, wiring and cable inlets for the AC power plug, network connection and POE network out. The Mini Computer Case case sits on the inside of your house, office or observatory and has a port for an RJ45 Cat6 cable that will connect to the camera module. The Mini Computer comes pre-configured with custom software to operate the cameras and fireball application. The Mini ComputerI is controlled by a browser based app that lets you review detections, change camera settings and view the live feed from the camera.

Camera Module

The AllSky6 Camera Module contains 6 Sony Starvis (starlight) IMX290 project cameras mounted on a custom cut acrylic plate and covered with a 250mm acrylic dome. The cameras are spaced 72 degrees apart in a pentagon formation creating 360 degree coverage of the sky in your area. A final 6th camera points up to capture the sky directly over head. The acrylic plate is attached to a PVC flange and PVC pipe that contains a custom built PCB that provides the cameras with power and network connections.

To keep the inside of the enclosure dry and cool, a climate control system has been developed. This system includes 2 thermostats, a micro dehumidifier, 3 fans, a 1amp Peltier heater/cooler, 2 heat sinks, a custom developed PCB board for easy wiring and an Arduino UNO micro-controller. The climate system checks the temperature inside the enclosure and compares it to the outside ambient temperature. The cooling unit is turned on anytime the internal temperature becomes 10 degrees hotter than the outside temperature. This keeps the internals cool, while staying above the ambient temperature, which helps prevent condensation from forming on top of the dome. A micro de-humidifier keeps the inside dry preventing internal condensation.
